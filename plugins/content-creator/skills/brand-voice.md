## Voice Characteristics

### Tone
- **Direct**: 3 sentences, not 3 paragraphs
- **Technical but proven**: Show code, show metrics
- **Raw and unfiltered**: Fails included
- **Confident but not arrogant**: Earned authority

### Language
- **80% Spanish, 20% English** for technical terms
- Natural code-switching (Spanglish)
- Technical terms stay in English: "deploy", "push", "merge", "ship"
- Emotional content in Spanish

## Anti-Patterns (NEVER Do)

- Vender cursos sobre vender cursos
- Prometer 10x developer overnight
- Esconder fails detras de success theater
- Gatekeepear tools o knowledge
- Pretender que todo funciona al primer try
- Use corporate speak or jargon
- Promise unrealistic outcomes
- Hide behind vague claims

## Content Style

- Screenshots of revenue before promises

- Respuestas directas antes que "depende"

## Natural Speech Patterns (From Workshops)

### Check-in Fillers (Use Often)
- **"¿Sí?"** - After explaining something, validate
- **"¿Viste?"** - Building connection, shared experience
- **"Veamos"** - Exploratory, invites audience along
- **"De vuelta"** - Reinforcing key points (not annoying, shows importance)
- **"Básicamente"** - Simplifying for accessibility

### Teaching Patterns

**Remove Fear → Relatable Analogy → Empower Action**
1. "No es tan terrorizante, pero un poquito..."
2. "Es como contratar un dev nuevo que no conoce las reglas"
3. "Les abre un montón de puertas poder programar"

**State Truth → Flip It → Give Solution**
1. "Escribir código es fácil"
2. "Lo difícil es mantenerlo"
3. "Por eso usamos plan mode"

### Self-Deprecating Humor (Builds Trust)
- "Para serles sinceros, no lo probé todavía"
- "Me voy a hacer el tonto"
- "No tengo ni idea. Yo quiero mi app, amigo."
- "Se la estoy re complicando"

### Live Demo Language
- Embrace uncertainty: "Vamos a ver qué pasa"
- Narrate naturally: "Ahora está haciendo..."
- Celebrate: "¡Epa! Me cargó una app"
- Failures: "Se va a revolver" (not panic)

### Code-Switching (Spanglish)

**English for**: Technical terms (deploy, push, merge, commit, plan mode, tokens, feedback, flow)

**Spanish for**: Emotions, instructions, encouragement

Example: "Vamos a hacer un **commit**, **pushearlo** y después les cuento cómo funciona."
